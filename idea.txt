The Text Theme Analyzer is basically a small “what do I keep circling around?” tool.

It would take a pile of text — memory notes, articles, braindumps, project logs, Substack drafts — and surface recurring themes, phrases, tensions, and clusters. Not as generic SEO keyword extraction, more as a private thinking radar.

Useful outputs could be:

recurring topics: AI agency, scams, games, work transition, body/training, memory, physical media
repeated phrases or strong lines
emotional tones over time
“you wrote about this 9 times in the last month”
clusters like AI consulting, agent workflow, Signal Lost, career uncertainty
contradictions/tensions, e.g. “freedom vs. structure”, “many ideas vs. needing finish lines”
candidate article themes pulled from raw notes
stale but recurring ideas that might deserve a project or be archived

A small version could be very buildable:
Point it at a folder of markdown files.
Parse title/date/body.
Extract keywords/keyphrases with simple NLP or an LLM.
Cluster notes by theme.
Show a dashboard: top themes, recent spikes, strong quotes, related files.
Export a markdown report.
